Wiki settings

{% csrf_token %} Wiki type:

A "Raw" wiki gives you complete control over your files: where you put them, what they're named, and what their contents are.

A "Managed HTML" wiki gives you a little less control over your files, by enforcing certain conventions and assumptions about where your files will go and what they contain:

  • All content is assumed to be HTML. You don't need to worry about naming your files with a ".html" extension.
  • You can edit your files with the Xinha WYSIWYG editor instead of typing in raw HTML manually.
  • All non-HTML files (images, plaintext, javascript, css...) will live in a "/b/" directory. When you upload files, they'll be put in that directory.
Using a "Managed HTML" wiki can be more convenient, as long as these assumptions don't get in your way.
